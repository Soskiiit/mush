Introduction in photogrammetry

Photogrammetry at all is "the science and technology of obtaining reliable information about physical objects and the environment through the process of recording, measuring and interpreting photographic images and patterns of electromagnetic radiant imagery and other phenomena", but, skipping this scary words, photogrammetry is the science of obtaining information about a three-dimensional - 3D object from two-dimensional - 2D data, in our case, a 3D model of an object from its photo. You can read more about photogrammetry here.

Your first project

Let's move from words to work, we'll begin from simple seven image dataset of a monitor, you can download it here.
  1. Create project by pressing "Create new project button" and select downloaded photos in the "Choose files" menu
  2. Now You can specify the project's name and set its status - if it's public, then it could be seen and downloaded by other users, otherwise, only You can see it. Also, You can delete any of your projects whenever You want by pressing "Delete" button in project settings
  3. When You're all set, press the "Upload model" button - after a few minutes of processing, it'll become available in "Personal projects" under your profile.
Congratulations, you have just created your first 3d model from photos!
